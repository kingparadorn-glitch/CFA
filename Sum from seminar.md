Executive Summary
The seminar introduces the fundamental roles and daily operations within investment strategy and wealth management, contrasting the buy-side and sell-side perspectives. The first speaker outlines the metrics used by asset management strategists to assess macroeconomic conditions, analyze market movements, and generate actionable trading ideas using Core (SAA) and Satellite (TAA) portfolios. The session then shifts to the sell-side, presenting wealth management as a lifelong partnership that extends beyond simple investing to address financial planning goals at every stage of life. Finally, the target demographic of the Mass Affluent sector is highlighted due to their strong purchasing power, volume, and complex financial planning needs.

Key Insights / Arguments
Strategists Must Filter Noise for Market-Moving Signals: A strategist's core responsibility is using tools like AI to distinguish key drivers from trivial economic metrics, identifying which specific factors actually move asset classes and international markets.

Exploiting Arbitrage in Market Disallocations: When widespread panic causes asset classes to move irrationally—such as safe-haven gold falling alongside risk assets during the 2025 trade war announcements—it uncovers clear tactical trading and allocation opportunities.

Balanced Core and Satellite Allocation: Portfolios should maintain a sturdy backbone with an 80% allocation to Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA) for long-term horizons of 5-10 years, balanced by a flexible 20% in Tactical Asset Allocation (TAA) to capture short-term, 3-to-12-month trends.

Wealth Management Encompasses a Complete Life Cycle: Comprehensive wealth management addresses the total life journey—encompassing wealth creation, lifestyle funding, retirement optimization, and inheritance generation—rather than just chasing investment returns.

Mass Affluent Clients Require High-Touch Financial Planning: Unlike ultra-high-net-worth individuals, the Mass Affluent group ($2M to $50M THB) presents the highest demand for rigorous, goal-oriented financial planning because their capital resources require careful lifecycle budgeting.
